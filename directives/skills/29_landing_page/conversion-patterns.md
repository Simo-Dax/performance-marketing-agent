# Conversion Patterns for Paid Meta Traffic

The strategy layer. Pulled from Unbounce 2024 Benchmark Report (41,000 pages, 464M visits, 57M conversions), Demand Curve, Imprint LA, KlientBoost, and other CRO sources.

## The conversion formula

From Demand Curve, the only formula worth memorizing:

```
Purchase Rate = (Desire + Confidence) / (Effort + Confusion)
```

Every section decision should answer one of those four. Does this section raise desire? Raise confidence? Lower effort? Lower confusion? If none, cut the section.

## The single biggest lever, message match

Message match is the alignment between the ad creative and the landing page. It is the single highest leverage conversion lever for paid traffic.

KlientBoost case, adding "Fully Free for Life" to both the ad and the landing page H1 produced a 66% lift.

Imprint LA, "Dedicated landing pages convert at ~15% for Meta ads, versus just 1 to 2% for a standard website homepage."

**Operational rule.** The H1 must contain at least one 3+ word phrase from the verbatim ad headline. If it does not, the page is broken regardless of how good the copy reads.

## Hero section rules

Above the fold, 7 elements:

1. **H1.** Message match to ad. Specific outcome, not vague.
2. **Subhead.** One sentence VOC outcome, polished. Or one sentence handling the largest objection.
3. **Hero visual.** Echoes ad creative. Same dominant subject, same color story.
4. **Primary CTA button.** Single verb intent. Price included if AOV is in the ad.
5. **Trust line inline.** Star rating + review count, OR customer count, OR press mention.
6. **No nav links off page.** Logo no link, no anchor nav.
7. **No multiple primary CTAs.** One destination only.

## Section ordering for cold Meta traffic

The order that wins (composite from Demand Curve, Pennock DTC framework, davila7 copywriting skill):

1. Hero (with social proof embedded)
2. Problem agitation (VOC pain quote)
3. Mechanism or How it works (3 to 4 numbered steps)
4. Benefit grid (3 to 6 outcomes, never features)
5. Social proof (testimonials with photos)
6. Comparison (us vs the alternative)
7. Detailed product info OR demo (transparency)
8. Founder story (why we built this)
9. FAQ (objection handler with verbatim VOC questions)
10. Guarantee (specific risk reversal)
11. Final CTA repeat (same destination)

Lead gen swaps comparison and detailed for "what you'll get" and mechanism in 3 steps. See `references/leadgen-route.md`.

## Word count and reading level

Unbounce 2024 data:

- 5th to 7th grade reading level converts at 12.9%
- Professional level converts at 2.1%
- Difficult words (3+ syllables) correlate with 24.3% decrease in conversion
- Sweet spot, 250 to 725 words total
- 50 to 140 difficult words maximum

**Operational rule.** Run a Hemingway style check on every paragraph. Flag anything above 7th grade. Rewrite.

## Mobile conversion rules

79% of paid Meta traffic is mobile. Mobile is the design target.

- Largest Contentful Paint under 2.5s (Google benchmark)
- 53% of mobile users abandon if a page takes over 3 seconds
- Body font minimum 16px
- Tap targets minimum 48px
- Sticky CTA bar after hero scroll (~15% lift, Section Store benchmark)
- Vertical pacing, alternate background tones to mark sections
- Never go more than 2 viewport heights without a CTA or proof element

## What's overrated, do not use

From CRO research consensus. These look good in case studies but do not move the needle on cold Meta traffic.

- Countdown timers on evergreen offers. Cold traffic does not believe them.
- Trust badge logos (Norton, McAfee, SSL). On a legitimate Shopify or Stripe checkout flow, these signal scam, not trust.
- "Limited supply" claims without a real number.
- Generic "join thousands of happy customers" with no source.
- Exit intent popups before first scroll completion. They block scroll.
- Live chat widgets on cold traffic. They distract from the CTA.
- Multiple CTA destinations. One page, one goal.

## What's underrated, use more

- Specific operational facts. "Ships from Copenhagen" beats "Fast shipping".
- Founder named guarantees. "Mike will refund you personally" beats "Money back guarantee".
- Comparison tables with named alternatives. "vs the drugstore version" beats "vs traditional options".
- Mobile sticky CTA bars on DTC. Consistent ~15% lift.
- FAQ pulled verbatim from customer questions. Pattern interrupts the lawyer voice.
- Verbatim pain quotes in problem agitation. The customer's wording beats yours.
- Single field email forms for lead gen. Multi field forms lose ~30% on cold traffic.

## Section count budget

Total sections, not counting hero and footer:

- DTC, 7 to 11 sections
- Lead gen short form (email capture), 5 to 7 sections
- Lead gen long form (demo book, high ticket), 8 to 11 sections

More than 12 sections, the page fatigues. Fewer than 5, the page is underbuilt for cold traffic that needs the convince.

## Pricing visibility rule

If the ad shows a price, the landing page shows the same price in the hero CTA button. "Add to Cart, $48" beats "Add to Cart". The price in the button removes one click of friction (no surprise at checkout) and pre qualifies tire kickers.

If the ad does not show a price, do not surface price in the hero CTA. Let the customer scroll to the detailed product section first to build desire before the number lands.
