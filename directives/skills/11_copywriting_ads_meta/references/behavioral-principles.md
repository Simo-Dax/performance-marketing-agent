# Behavioral Principles for Meta Ads

Use these principles to shape the persuasion angle of ad copy. Each principle works best at specific funnel stages. Multiple principles can be combined in a single ad, but one should be dominant.

---

## Authority

**What it is:** People trust and follow credible experts, institutions, or recognized figures.

**When to use:** Consideration, Conversion. When the product has endorsements, certifications, expert backing, or industry recognition.

**How to apply in ad copy:**
- Reference specific credentials, certifications, or awards
- Cite expert opinions or recommendations (real ones only, use [INSERIRE DATO] as placeholder if needed)
- Mention years of experience, number of clients served, or professional affiliations
- Use language that signals competence: "sviluppato da", "raccomandato da", "certificato"

**Tone:** Confident, professional. Not boastful.

**Example angles:**
- "[N] professionisti lo consigliano"
- "Sviluppato con [ente/esperto]"
- "Il metodo usato da [gruppo autorevole]"

---

## Social Proof

**What it is:** People look to others' behavior to determine what's correct or desirable.

**When to use:** Consideration, Conversion, Remarketing. When the product has reviews, testimonials, user numbers, or community traction.

**How to apply in ad copy:**
- Quote real testimonials (or use [INSERIRE TESTIMONIANZA] placeholder)
- Cite user numbers: "Oltre [N] clienti soddisfatti"
- Reference community behavior: "Il più scelto da..."
- Show ratings, review scores, or awards from user communities
- Use specificity: exact numbers and names are more convincing than vague claims

**Tone:** Warm, relatable. Let others speak.

**Example angles:**
- Direct quote from a customer
- "[N] persone hanno già scelto [prodotto]"
- "Valutato [X]/5 da [N] recensioni"

---

## Scarcity

**What it is:** People value what's limited in availability. Perceived scarcity increases desire.

**When to use:** Conversion, Remarketing. When there's a genuine limit (stock, seats, time-limited offer).

**How to apply in ad copy:**
- Reference limited quantities: "Solo [N] posti disponibili"
- Highlight exclusivity: "Riservato a...", "Edizione limitata"
- Mention closing deadlines: "Disponibile fino al [data]"
- Never fake scarcity. If the product isn't actually limited, don't claim it is.

**Tone:** Direct, factual. Urgency without panic.

**Example angles:**
- "Ultimi [N] disponibili"
- "Non tornerà in stock"
- "Solo per i primi [N] iscritti"

---

## Urgency

**What it is:** Time pressure compresses the decision timeline and reduces procrastination.

**When to use:** Conversion, Remarketing. When there's a real deadline or time-sensitive benefit.

**How to apply in ad copy:**
- Set a clear deadline: "L'offerta scade il [data]"
- Use countdown language: "Mancano [N] giorni", "Ultime ore"
- Tie urgency to a consequence: "Dopo [data], il prezzo torna a [€]"
- Combine with scarcity for maximum effect, but only if both are real

**Tone:** Energetic, direct. Not desperate.

**Example angles:**
- "Solo fino a [data]"
- "Oggi: [sconto]%. Domani: prezzo pieno."
- "Non aspettare: [conseguenza del ritardo]"

---

## Loss Aversion

**What it is:** People feel losses more strongly than equivalent gains. The fear of losing something is a stronger motivator than the prospect of gaining it.

**When to use:** Remarketing, Conversion. When the user has already shown interest (retargeting) or when the cost of inaction is tangible.

**How to apply in ad copy:**
- Frame the message around what they'll miss, not what they'll gain
- Reference sunk costs: "Hai già [azione precedente], non fermarti ora"
- Highlight what competitors or peers are gaining while they hesitate
- Use "non perdere" instead of "approfitta di"

**Tone:** Empathetic but firm. Not manipulative.

**Example angles:**
- "Stai perdendo [X] ogni giorno senza [prodotto]"
- "I tuoi concorrenti lo stanno già usando"
- "Non lasciare che [opportunità] ti sfugga"

---

## Friction Removal

**What it is:** Reducing perceived barriers (cost, effort, risk, complexity) makes action easier.

**When to use:** Conversion, Remarketing. When the product has a free trial, guarantee, easy setup, or no-commitment option.

**How to apply in ad copy:**
- Address the specific friction the target audience feels
- Offer guarantees: "Soddisfatti o rimborsati", "Prova gratuita"
- Simplify the action: "In 2 minuti", "Senza carta di credito"
- Pre-answer the "what if it doesn't work" question

**Tone:** Reassuring, low-pressure. Remove doubt.

**Example angles:**
- "Zero rischi. Prova per [N] giorni gratis."
- "Configuralo in [N] minuti"
- "Nessun vincolo. Cancella quando vuoi."

---

## Principle Selection by Funnel Stage

| Funnel Stage | Primary Principles | Secondary Principles |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | (Not principle-driven, use pain points / desires / USPs) | Authority for credibility hooks |
| Consideration | Authority, Social Proof | Friction Removal |
| Conversion | Scarcity, Urgency, Loss Aversion, Friction Removal | Social Proof |
| Remarketing | Loss Aversion, Social Proof, Scarcity, Urgency | Friction Removal |

## Combining Principles

You can layer principles in a single ad. Common effective combinations:
- **Social Proof + Urgency**: "[N] persone lo hanno già acquistato. L'offerta scade domani."
- **Scarcity + Friction Removal**: "Ultimi [N] posti. Prova senza rischi."
- **Authority + Loss Aversion**: "I professionisti del settore lo consigliano. Non restare indietro."
- **Loss Aversion + Urgency**: "Ogni giorno senza [prodotto] ti costa [X]. Agisci ora."

Keep one principle dominant. The second should support, not compete.
